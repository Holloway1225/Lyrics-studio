Tell Me The Story

Answer as many as you can. The more specific you are, the better the song. Skip anything that doesn't apply.

1. WHAT HAPPENED?
The core event. One or two sentences. Be specific about what actually occurred.
2. WHERE WERE YOU AND WHAT DID YOU SEE FIRST?
The first physical thing you noticed. A specific object, smell, sound, or image.
3. WHAT DID YOU DO?
Your physical actions in the moment. Not how you felt — what you actually did.
4. WHAT WAS THE HARDEST MOMENT?
The single worst second. A specific detail from that moment — something you saw, heard, or said.
5. HOW DID IT END OR WHERE ARE YOU NOW?
What happened after. Or where you are emotionally now looking back.
6. ONE DETAIL ONLY YOU WOULD KNOW
Something small and specific that nobody else would think to include. A habit, an object, a phrase she used, a smell, anything.
7. YOUR LINES — PROTECTED & FEATURED
Any lines you've already written that must appear verbatim in the final song.
